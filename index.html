LIFE LESSONS LEARNT WHILE LEARNING TO CODE

View summary of all lessons

Follow your own learning path because it is the only way to simplify your journey to becoming the best programmer you can be. Following a path laid out by someone else makes the journey unnecessarily difficult. The key(magic) is to take whatever resource you have access to and create your own learning path. In other words; master the concepts your way in a self-paced manner. As you faithfully do this you will gradually realize that your unique thinking patterns, thoughts, and your creative spirit will come to bare and lead you to your goal far easier than by following someone else's path.

In line with the first point. Don't depend solely on others doing things for you. Try your best to do things for yourself. Do all in your power to get things done for yourself. When you allow others to do things for you or solve problems for you it's their solution and not yours. Consequently it becomes harder for you to internalize that solution and make it your own. When you think of or come up with a solution to a problem by yourself it makes you depend less on others to get problems solved. This way of life helps you increase your power in this world. As a bonus, when you are comfortable solving your own problems yourself you easily pick up tips and tricks from how other people go about solving thier own problems. This you can incorporate into your personal problem solving "arsenal".

Understanding a problem is extremely important to being able to solve it. It is pertinent to state that trying to solve a problem that has not really been understood easily leads to frustration and time wasting. Before allocating time and resources with the goal of solving a problem or meeting a need, it is wise to first of all ensure that the problem or need is well understood. It is better to go after a problem that one has a deep understanding of than to go after a problem that one is only interested in solving without a deep understanding. It is important to restress this point. When it comes to choosing between solving a problem that a person is interested in (no matter the extent of ones interest) as opposed to a problem that one has a deep understanding of, the wisest thing to do is to go after the problem that is properly understood.

Trying to get into tech the conventional way may make it impossible to ever get into tech. Try an unconventinal approach; be creative. Getting into tech the unconventional way is even easier (but of course would still require alot of hard work).

Learn to think for yourself. Learn to figure out for yourself how things work. You are at the mercy of others when they figure things out for you, hire you, or add you to their team. It is them and not you that call the shots, make decisions and get the larger share of rewards. They will also do away with you the moment you are no longer needed or they find someone better than you, often, with no regards for your welfare or emotion. Thinking and figuring things out for yourself makes it easy to transition from been unemployed to getting a job; starting a career and ultimately fulfilling your life's task. Make it your overaching goal in life to think for yourself. Thinking plays a crucial role in achieving all round freedom in life.

Sometimes to get an idea you need to focus on what already exists. The services and products already available when observed/analysed can help you think of ways to improve upon them. Better still it makes it easy to come up with something different or new as opposed to getting a new idea or way of doing things from no where. N.B: Sometimes it's when you have looked for a very long time at code you have written that you begin to get ideas to improve it.
